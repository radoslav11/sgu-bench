**Key insight about cutting:**
A straight line divides a convex quadrilateral into two equal areas, each containing one candle. 

For a pie that can be cut: the cut is valid if there exists a line that:
- Divides the quadrilateral into two equal-area pieces
- Each piece contains exactly one candle
- The line doesn't pass through a candle

**Practical approach:** A bisecting line separates c1,c2 iff the continuous family of bisecting lines includes one that strictly separates them. So: check if the midpoint of the two candles is inside (or on boundary of) the quadrilateral, and compute the bisecting line through that midpoint.
