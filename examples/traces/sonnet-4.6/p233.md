Looking at this problem, I need to find point C on a circle such that angle ACB is maximized.

**Key insight**: The angle ACB is maximized when C lies on the circle passing through A, B, and C that is tangent to the given circle from inside (or where the circumscribed circle of triangle ACB is smallest). 

By the inscribed angle theorem, for a fixed chord AB, the angle ACB is larger when C is on a circle through A and B with smaller radius (since the inscribed angle is inversely related to the circumradius: angle = arcsin(AB/(2R_circ))).

So I need to find the point C on the big circle such that the circumradius of triangle ACB is minimized, which means finding where the circle through A, B, C has the smallest possible radius while C is on the big circle.

**Another approach**: The locus of points where angle ACB = θ is a circular arc. As θ increases, the circle through A, B, C gets smaller. The maximum angle occurs when the circumscribed circle of ACB is tangent internally to the given circle.

The center of the circumscribed circle of ACB lies on the perpendicular bisector of AB. Let me parameterize: the perpendicular bisector of AB, find where a circle centered on this line passing through A and B is internally tangent to the big circle.

Let M be midpoint of AB, let the perpendicular bisector direction be unit vector **u**. Centers of circles through A,B lie at M + t*u for parameter t. The radius of such circle is r(t) = sqrt(|MA|² + t²).

For internal tangency with the big circle (center O, radius R): distance from O to center of small circle + r(t) = R (if small circle inside big circle).

Let d(t) = |O - (M + t*u)|. We need d(t) + sqrt(|MA|² + t²) = R.

To minimize r(t) = sqrt(|MA|² + t²), we want to minimize t², so t=0 if possible, but subject to the tangency constraint.

Actually, we want the circle through A,B with minimum radius that fits inside the big circle. The minimum radius circle through A,B that is inside has its center at some t on the perpendicular bisector such that the circle is tangent to the big circle.

Let me set up coordinates: Let's work in a translated system where I find t by solving:
d(t) + sqrt(|MA|² + t²) = R

The optimal C is the point of tangency, which is along the line from O to the circumcenter of ACB, at distance R from O.

Let me find t by numerical search (or analytically). The function f(t) = d(t) + sqrt(|MA|² + t²) - R = 0. There might be two solutions; pick the one with smaller r(t).

Actually I should minimize r(t) subject to d(t) + r(t) ≤ R. The minimum r(t) subject to the constraint d(t) + r(t) = R occurs at the t where d/dt[r(t)] = 0 or at boundary. But we want minimum r(t) on the feasible set... actually minimum r means maximum angle. The constraint is that C (point of tangency) is on the big circle and the circumscribed circle fits inside.

The point C is at: C = circumcenter + r(t) * (unit vector from circumcenter toward big circle's closest point on the line O-circumcenter).

C = circumcenter + r(t) * (O - circumcenter)/|O-circumcenter| * (-1)... C is at distance R from O, on the line from O through circumcenter, on the far side.

Let me just do ternary search on t to minimize r(t) subject to d(t)+r(t)=R... actually ternary search on the angle of C on the big circle to maximize angle ACB directly.

```cpp
#include <bits/stdc++.h>
using namespace std;

int main(){
    int N;
    scanf("%d",&N);
    while(N--){
        double x0,y0,R,xa,ya,xb,yb;
        scanf("%lf%lf%lf%lf%lf%lf%lf",&x0,&y0,&R,&xa,&ya,&xb,&yb);
        // ternary search on angle
        double lo=0, hi=2*M_PI;
        for(int i=0;i<200;i++){
            double m1=lo+(hi-lo)/3, m2=hi-(hi-lo)/3;
            double cx1=x0+R*cos(m1), cy1=y0+R*sin(m1);
            double cx2=x0+R*cos(m2), cy2=y0+R*sin(m2);
            auto angle=[&](double cx,double cy){
                double ax=xa-cx,ay=ya-cy,bx=xb-cx,by=yb-cy;
                return acos((ax*bx+ay*by)/(sqrt(ax*ax+ay*ay)*sqrt(bx*bx+by*by)));
            };
            if(angle(cx1,cy1)<angle(cx2,cy2)) lo=m1; else hi=m2;
        }
        double mid=(lo+hi)/2;
        printf("%.6f %.6f\n",x0+R*cos(mid),y0+R*sin(mid));
    }
}
```
