The key insight is that I need to distribute operations to eliminate brackets. The precedence is:
- Highest: `!` (negation)
- Middle: `&` (conjunction)
- Low: `||`, `<=>`, `=>`, `#`

For bracket-free output, I need to represent the expression in a form where the precedence rules naturally give the right evaluation order.

The approach: Parse the expression into a tree, then convert to a bracket-free string representation by distributing operations.

Actually, the simplest approach is to represent everything at the level of low-priority operators. Each expression can be represented as a sum (low-priority operations) of products (& chains) of literals (negated variables or just variables).

Actually, let me convert the expression to use only `!`, `&`, `||` (eliminating `=>`, `<=>`, `#`):

- `A # B` = `(A & !B) || (!A & B)` (XOR)

Then I need to distribute `&` over `||` to get DNF (disjunctive normal form), which is naturally bracket-free since `&` has higher priority than `||`.
