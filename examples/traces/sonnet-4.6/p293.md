Looking at this problem, let me understand the mechanics:

1. He writes 2n-1 letters total
2. They alternate turns: He writes, then Qc acts, He writes, then Qc acts, ...
3. After He writes letter 1: Qc passes (turn 1 for Qc - odd, must be palindrome)
4. After He writes letter 2: Qc can swap (turn 2 for Qc - even, no constraint)
5. After He writes letter 3: Qc can swap (turn 3 for Qc - odd, must be palindrome)
...

So after He writes letter k, Qc takes their k-th turn. Qc's odd turns are 1,3,5,... meaning after He writes letters 1,3,5,... (odd-indexed letters). At those points the string must be a palindrome.

After He writes letter 1 (length 1): always palindrome, Qc passes.
After He writes letter 3 (length 3): Qc made one swap after letter 2. String of length 3 must be palindrome.
After He writes letter 5 (length 5): Qc made one swap after letter 4. String of length 5 must be palindrome.

So at each odd-length string (lengths 1,3,5,...,2n-1), after at most one swap, the string must be a palindrome.

A string of odd length 2k+1 is a palindrome if positions i and 2k+1-i+1 match, for i=1..k, and the middle can be anything.

Strategy: At each even step (between odd-length strings), we have one swap to fix the string.

When going from length 2k-1 (palindrome) to length 2k+1: He adds two characters. The new string has the old palindrome in the middle plus one character at each end? No - He adds one character at a time to the end.

After length 2k-1 is a palindrome, He adds character at position 2k (Qc can swap once), then He adds at position 2k+1 (must be palindrome after Qc's swap which is the odd turn).

Wait - turn order: He writes, then Qc acts. After He writes position 2k, Qc acts (even turn, no palindrome constraint). After He writes position 2k+1, Qc acts (odd turn, must be palindrome).

So between palindrome at length 2k-1 and required palindrome at length 2k+1, Qc gets two swaps (one after position 2k, one after 2k+1).

For a string of length 2k+1 to be a palindrome, we need s[i] == s[2k+2-i] for i=1..k. With at most 2 swaps total to fix it from the current state.
