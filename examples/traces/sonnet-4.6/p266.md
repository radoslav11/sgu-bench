Looking at this problem, I need to find the area of a sphere's surface visible from at least one of two scanners.

**Key insight:** From a point outside a sphere, the visible portion of the sphere is a spherical cap. The two scanners each see a cap, and I need the area of their union.

**Spherical cap from one scanner:**
If scanner is at distance `d` from center, and sphere has radius `R`, then the visible cap has a half-angle `α` where `sin(α) = R/d`, so the tangent line from the scanner touches the sphere at angle `α` from the line connecting center to scanner.

The spherical cap area is `2πR²(1 - cos(α))` where `cos(α) = sqrt(1 - R²/d²)`.

Wait, let me reconsider. The cap visible from the scanner: a point P on sphere is visible from scanner S if the line PS doesn't intersect the sphere (except at P). The boundary is when the line is tangent. If the angle at center between the center-to-scanner direction and center-to-P is `θ`, then visibility condition is `cos(θ) ≥ R/d` (i.e., `θ ≤ arccos(R/d)`).

So `θ_max = arccos(R/d)`, and cap area = `2πR²(1 - cos(θ_max)) = 2πR²(1 - R/d)`.

**Union area:** Area(A) + Area(B) - Area(A∩B).
