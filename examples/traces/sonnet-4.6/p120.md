Looking at this problem, I need to find all vertices of a regular N-gon given two vertices (N1 and N2) with their coordinates.

**Approach:**
1. In a regular N-gon, all vertices are equally spaced on a circle.
2. The angle between consecutive vertices (from center) is 2π/N.
3. Given two vertices, I can find the center and radius of the circumscribed circle.

**Finding the center:**
- The center is equidistant from all vertices.
- Vertex k is at angle θ_k from the center, where θ_k = θ_0 + (k-1) * 2π/N (clockwise, so actually θ_k = θ_0 - (k-1) * 2π/N in standard math orientation, but let me think carefully).

Let me parameterize: Vertex i is at center + R*(cos(α + (i-1)*φ), sin(α + (i-1)*φ)) where φ = -2π/N (clockwise means angle decreases as index increases in standard coordinates).

So vertex N1 is at: (cx + R*cos(α + (N1-1)*φ), cy + R*sin(α + (N1-1)*φ))
And vertex N2 is at: (cx + R*cos(α + (N2-1)*φ), cy + R*sin(α + (N2-1)*φ))

The difference in angle between N1 and N2 is (N2-N1)*φ = (N2-N1)*(-2π/N).

Let me denote the known angle difference: δ = (N2-N1) * (-2π/N).

Vector from N1 to N2: (x2-x1, y2-y1) = R*(cos(α+(N2-1)*φ) - cos(α+(N1-1)*φ), ...)

Let θ1 = α + (N1-1)*φ. Then N2's angle is θ1 + δ.

The midpoint of N1 and N2 is: center + R*(cos(θ1)+cos(θ1+δ))/2, center + R*(sin(θ1)+sin(θ1+δ))/2.

The center of the chord from N1 to N2 lies along the direction perpendicular to that chord, at distance d from the chord midpoint, where d can be computed from the chord length and the angle δ.

Chord length L = |N2-N1|. Also L = 2R*sin(|δ|/2).

The perpendicular from chord midpoint to center: distance = R*cos(|δ|/2).

The perpendicular direction (there are two choices): rotate (x2-x1, y2-y1) by 90 degrees.

The center is at: midpoint ± (R*cos(δ/2)) * perpendicular_unit.

But we need to determine the sign. The vertices are clockwise, so the center should be on the left side of the directed chord from N1 to N2 (if δ is negative, i.e., N2 > N1).

Let me think: with clockwise ordering, moving from N1 to N2 (increasing index), the center is to the left of the direction N1→N2 when going clockwise... actually let me just try both and pick the correct one based on the sign of δ.

For clockwise polygon: rotating N1→N2 vector by -90 degrees (clockwise) gives direction toward center when N2 comes after N1 (N2 > N1, δ < 0... let me just implement and test).

Actually: perpendicular to (dx,dy) rotated +90 is (-dy, dx), rotated -90 is (dy, -dx).

For a clockwise polygon with vertices going clockwise, the center is to the right of each edge... Let me just try both signs and check with the sample.
