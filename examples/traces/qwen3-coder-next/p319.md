To solve this problem, we need to determine the areas of all connected white regions (facets) on a canvas after drawing several non-intersecting rectangular frames. The frames are drawn as borders, and the white regions are the areas enclosed by these frames or the canvas itself. The solution involves processing the frames to identify nested relationships, computing the areas of the resulting regions, and sorting them in non-decreasing order.

### Approach
1. **Input Reading and Initialization**: Read the number of frames \( N \) and the canvas dimensions \( W \) (width) and \( H \) (height). For each frame, read the coordinates of two opposite corners and compute the bounding rectangle (minimum and maximum x and y coordinates) and its area.
2. **Sorting Frames**: Sort the frames based on their minimum x-coordinate in ascending order. For frames with the same minimum x-coordinate, sort by maximum x-coordinate in descending order, then by minimum y-coordinate in ascending order, and finally by maximum y-coordinate in descending order. This sorting helps in efficiently determining nested relationships.
3. **Building Nesting Tree**: Use a stack to process the sorted frames. For each frame, pop frames from the stack until the top frame strictly contains the current frame (i.e., the top frame's x and y boundaries are strictly outside the current frame's boundaries). The top frame after popping becomes the parent of the current frame in the nesting tree.
4. **Computing Region Areas**: For each frame, compute the area of its inner region (annulus) as the area of its bounding rectangle minus the sum of the areas of its nested child frames. The outermost region (outside all frames) is computed as the total canvas area minus the sum of the areas of all outermost frames (those with no parent).
5. **Output**: Collect all region areas (outermost region and annuli for each frame), sort them in non-decreasing order, and output the result.

2. **Sorting**: Frames are sorted to facilitate nesting detection. The sorting is primarily by increasing minimum x-coordinate, with secondary sorts to prioritize frames that are more likely to contain others.
