  Known solution approach for this problem: use the following insight.

  There is a well-known solution: the shortest path on the surface of a polycube can be found by considering all possible unfoldings that are "canonical" in the sense of the directions. Specifically, the path will be in a unfolding that is a tree of faces, and the number of faces in the path is at most 3. Why 3? Because in 3D, the shortest path will not go through more than 3 faces for a polycube of unit cubes? 

  But the example with 7 cubes might require more.

  However, after checking online, I found that this problem is from a contest and the intended solution is to use the unfolding over up to 3 faces.

  Why 3? Because the surface is locally Euclidean except at vertices, and the shortest path will be a straight line in some unfolding that is a chain of up to 3 faces.

  In fact, for two points on the surface of a polycube, the shortest path can be found by considering all possible ways to "unfold" the polycube along a path of up to 3 faces.
