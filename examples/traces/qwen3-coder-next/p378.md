To solve this problem, we need to help Peter find the minimal time required to save himself by either reaching the boat or the shore of the lake. The lake is represented as an N x M grid where each cell has a flow velocity vector. Peter can move in any of the five possible vectors (up, down, left, right, or stay) at the center of any cell, and his actual movement is the sum of the flow velocity and his chosen movement vector. The goal is to determine the minimal time Peter needs to reach the boat or the shore.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**:
   - Peter starts at a given position, and the boat starts at another position.
   - Peter can move from the center of one cell to another cell's center in a straight line determined by the combined flow and his movement vectors.
   - The time to move to an adjacent cell depends on the magnitude of the combined velocity vector. For example:
     - If the combined velocity is (0, 1), moving down, the time to reach the next cell down is 1 minute.
     - If the combined velocity is (0, 2), the time is 0.5 minutes.
     - For diagonal moves (e.g., (1, 1)), the time is 1 minute (since the max component is 1).
   - Peter is saved if he reaches the boat or any shore cell (i.e., any cell on the boundary of the grid).

2. **Algorithm Selection**:
   - **Dijkstra's Algorithm**: We use Dijkstra's algorithm to explore the grid, treating each cell as a node. The distance to each cell is the minimal time required to reach it from the starting position.
   - **Priority Queue**: A priority queue is used to always expand the cell with the minimal current time.
   - **Movement Handling**: For each cell, we consider all possible movement vectors Peter can choose. The next cell is calculated by adding the flow vector and the chosen movement vector. The time to move to the next cell is derived from the combined velocity's magnitude.

3. **Complexity Analysis**:
   - **Time Complexity**: O(N * M * 5 * log(N * M)), where N and M are the grid dimensions. Each cell is processed once, and for each cell, we consider up to 5 movement vectors. The priority queue operations take logarithmic time.
   - **Space Complexity**: O(N * M) for storing the distance matrix and the priority queue.
