                             if "brother", then P_{i+1} is the brother of P_i -> so they share a parent (and we know from the assignment which parent's side, but actually we don't need to know which parent, we only know they are siblings).
                             similarly for "sister", "uncle", "aunt", "nephew", "niece".

                        But for building the graph, we need to know the parents of the siblings.

                        Here's the key: the only marriages that are forced are between the parents of a common child. And the only children that are mentioned are the ones in the chain and their parents as defined by the chain.

                        How to get the parents of a person in the chain?

                          - If the person is P_i, and the next relation is "father" or "mother", then we know one parent (P_{i+1}).
                          - If the previous relation is "son" or "daughter", then we know one parent (P_{i-1}).

                        But what about the other parent? It is not specified. However, for marriage, we need to know who is married to whom.

                        The problem says: we can assume the family tree has no cycles, and we are to find min and max over all possible trees. So for the minimal distance, we would assume that whenever two people could be married, they are (to create 0-weight edges), and for the maximal distance, we assume they are not married (if not forced).

                        But what is forced?

                          - The only forced marriages are between the parents of a common child. And a common child is any person for whom we know two parents.

                        However, in the chain, for a person P_i, we might know:

                          - One parent from the next relation (if the next relation is "father" or "mother") or from the previous relation (if the previous relation is "son" or "daughter").

                        Specifically, for a person P_i:

                          - If i < k and relations[i] is "father" or "mother", then we know one parent: P_{i+1}.
                          - If i > 0 and relations[i-1] is "son" or "daughter", then we know one parent: P_{i-1}.

                        If we know two parents for P_i, then they must be married.

                        But what if we only know one parent? However, the other parent might be a new person not in the chain.
