We are given N (number of cities) and M (number of airlines)
 Then an N x N matrix, where matrix[i][j] (for i != j) is the airline company that owns the flight between city i and city j.

 Note: The flight is undirected (symmetric), and the diagonal is 0.

 The president wants to buy some set S of airlines (with |S| <= (M+1)//2) such that in the graph formed by only the flights of airlines in S, the distance between any two cities is at most 2 (i.e., at most 3 vertices: A -> C -> B or A -> B directly).

 In other words, the graph with edges only from airlines in S must have diameter at most 2.

 If no such set exists, output -1.

The goal is to select a subset of airlines such that the subgraph formed by their edges has a diameter of at most 2. The size of this subset must not exceed \((M + 1) // 2\).

3. **Pairwise Check**: If no single city satisfies the condition, we check pairs of cities. Buying all airlines incident to either city in the pair ensures that any city not directly connected to one city in the pair can be connected via the other city, maintaining a diameter of at most 2.

4. **Algorithm**:
   - **Single Vertex Check**: For each city, collect the distinct airlines of its incident flights. If the count is within the limit, output these airlines.
