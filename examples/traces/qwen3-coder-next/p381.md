We are given a bidirected graph and need to determine if we can make it "directed" via elementary transformations (flipping signs at vertices) with minimum transformations.

Recall:
- A bidirected edge e between u and v has signs (d1, d2) = (ω(e, u), ω(e, v)) ∈ {-1,1}^2.
- An elementary transformation at vertex v flips ω(e, v) for every edge e incident to v.

We want the graph to become "directed", meaning: for each edge e = (u, v), we want (ω(e, u), ω(e, v)) to be either (-1, +1) or (+1, -1). That is, the two signs must be opposite.

Note: Currently, each edge has signs (d1, d2) ∈ {-1,1}^2. There are four possibilities:
- (-1, -1): both "incoming" in directed graph terms? But in directed graph, one must be source (-1) and one sink (+1).
- (+1, +1): both "outgoing"
- (-1, +1): good (like u→v)
- (+1, -1): good (like u←v)

So edges with same signs (both -1 or both +1) are "bad", and we need to fix them.
