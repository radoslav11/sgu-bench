                In general, by the Cauchy-Davenport theorem, for a subset S of Z/pZ, 
                  |S+S| >= min(p, 2|S|-1).

                We want S ∩ (S+S) ≠ ∅. 
                  If |S| > p/2, then |S+S| = p (if |S| > p/2, then 2|S| > p, so |S+S|>=p), so S+S = Z/pZ, and since S is nonempty, there is intersection.
