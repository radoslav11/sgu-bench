To solve this problem, we need to maximize the number of days a truck can operate in a city's road network while cleaning all historical roads completely. The truck starts at junction A and ends at junction B each day, traversing roads only if they have snow, removing one ton per traversal. Historical roads must be entirely cleaned (snow reduced to zero), while regular roads can have leftover snow. The solution involves modeling the problem as a maximum flow problem with lower bounds on historical edges and then decomposing the flow into valid paths.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: The city is modeled as a directed graph where each road has a certain amount of snow. Historical roads must be traversed exactly their snow amount times, while regular roads can be traversed up to their snow amount times. The goal is to find the maximum number of paths from A to B that satisfy these constraints.

2. **Flow Model with Lower Bounds**: 
   - **Lower Bounds**: For historical roads, the flow must exactly equal their snow amount. For regular roads, the flow can be zero up to their snow amount.
   - **Transformation**: Convert the problem into a standard max-flow problem by:
     - Computing node imbalances (D[v] = inflow - outflow from historical roads).
     - Adding a super source (S) and super sink (T) to handle imbalances.
     - Adding an edge from B to A with infinite capacity to model circulation, allowing net flow from A to B.
   - **Feasibility Check**: The max flow from S to T must saturate all edges from S to ensure feasibility.
   - **Maximize Net Flow**: After ensuring feasibility, compute additional flow from A to B in the residual graph to maximize the total days.

3. **Path Decomposition**: Once the total flow (F) is determined, decompose it into F paths from A to B using BFS for each path, adjusting residual capacities accordingly.

3. **Node Imbalance (D)**: Computes the difference between inflow and outflow required for historical roads at each node.
4. **Flow Network Construction**:
   - **Super Source (S) and Sink (T)**: Connect nodes with positive D to S and negative D to T to balance imbalances.
   - **Circulation Edge (B to A)**: Allows net flow from A to B, with infinite capacity.
5. **Feasibility Check**: Ensures the max flow from S to T saturates all demand edges.
6. **Maximize Net Flow**: Computes additional flow from A to B in the residual graph to maximize the total days (F = F1 + F2).
7. **Path Decomposition**: Decomposes the total flow into valid paths using BFS, adjusting residual capacities for each path found.
8. **Output**: Prints the number of days (F) followed by each path from A to B.

This approach efficiently models the problem as a max-flow problem with lower bounds, ensures feasibility, maximizes the number of days, and outputs the solution paths.
