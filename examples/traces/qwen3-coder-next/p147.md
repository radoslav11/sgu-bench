To solve this problem, we need to determine whether the black-white king can intercept either the black or white king before they meet (i.e., before they occupy adjacent cells). If interception is possible, we find the minimal number of moves required; otherwise, we compute the minimal total moves required for the black and white kings to meet.

### Approach
1. **Problem Analysis**: 
   - The chessboard is of size \( N \times N \), but the actual size isn't directly used since coordinates are within bounds.
   - Three kings are present: black, white, and black-white. The black and white kings move to become adjacent (Chebyshev distance ≤ 1), while the black-white king aims to intercept one of them by moving to their cell before they meet.
   - The move order is: white king, then black king, then black-white king. The black and white kings choose the shortest path to meet, moving optimally without considering the black-white king.

2. **Key Insight**:
   - The minimal number of rounds (each round consists of moves by white, black, and black-white kings) for the black and white kings to meet is \( T = \lfloor d / 2 \rfloor \), where \( d \) is the Chebyshev distance between them (i.e., \( d = \max(|P1 - P2|, |Q1 - Q2|) \)).
   - The black-white king can intercept the black king in \( k \) moves if \( D \leq 3k \), where \( D \) is the Chebyshev distance from the black-white king to the black king. Similarly, it can intercept the white king if \( E \leq 3k \), where \( E \) is the Chebyshev distance to the white king.
   - The minimal \( k \) for interception is \( \lceil D/3 \rceil \) or \( \lceil E/3 \rceil \), whichever is smaller. If this \( k \) is less than \( T \), interception is possible; otherwise, the kings meet in \( 2T \) total moves (since each king moves \( T \) times).

```cpp
    long long D = max(abs(p1 - p3), abs(q1 - q3));
    long long E = max(abs(p2 - p3), abs(q2 - q3));

    long long k0 = min((D + 2) / 3, (E + 2) / 3);

    if (k0 < T) {
        cout << "YES" << endl;
        cout << k0 << endl;
    } else {
        cout << "NO" << endl;
        cout << 2 * T << endl;
    }

    return 0;
}
```

### Explanation
1. **Input Reading**: The code reads the board size \( N \) (unused) and the coordinates of the three kings.
2. **Chebyshev Distance Calculation**: Computes \( d \), the Chebyshev distance between the black and white kings, which determines the minimal rounds \( T = \lfloor d / 2 \rfloor \) needed for them to meet.
3. **Interception Check**: For the black-white king, calculates \( D \) (distance to black king) and \( E \) (distance to white king). The minimal moves \( k0 \) for interception is \( \min(\lceil D/3 \rceil, \lceil E/3 \rceil) \).
4. **Decision Making**: If \( k0 < T \), interception is possible in \( k0 \) moves (output "YES" and \( k0 \)). Otherwise, the kings meet in \( 2T \) total moves (output "NO" and \( 2T \)).

This approach efficiently checks for interception feasibility by leveraging Chebyshev distance properties and simple arithmetic, ensuring optimal performance even for large \( N \).
