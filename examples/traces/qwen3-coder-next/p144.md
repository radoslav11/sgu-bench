We are given two integers X and Y (representing the time interval [X, Y] in hours) and a real number Z (the maximum waiting time in minutes).

The two people will arrive at some time between X and Y (inclusive). Let the arrival times be A and B, which are uniformly distributed over [X, Y].

We want the probability that |A - B| <= Z (in minutes). However, note that A and B are in hours, so we must convert everything to the same unit.

Let T = Y - X (in hours). Then the total time interval length is T hours, which is 60*T minutes.

The condition for meeting is: |A - B| <= Z (minutes).

We can model this geometrically. Consider the square in the plane with corners at (X, X), (X, Y), (Y, X), and (Y, Y). The total area of the square is (Y-X)^2.

The condition |A - B| <= Z (minutes) is equivalent to |A - B| <= Z/60 hours.

So we require: |A - B| <= (Z/60).

The region where they meet is the set of points (a, b) in [X, Y] x [Y, X] such that |a - b| <= (Z/60).

Geometrically, this is the area between the lines b = a - (Z/60) and b = a + (Z/60), within the square [X, Y] x [X, Y].

The probability is then: 
   1 - (area of the two triangles where |a-b| > Z/60) / (total area)

The total area is (Y-X)^2.

The region where they do not meet consists of two right triangles:
1. Below the line b = a - (Z/60): This triangle has legs of length (T - Z/60) if T >= Z/60, otherwise the entire square is covered by the meeting region.
2. Above the line b = a + (Z/60): Similarly.

But note: if Z/60 >= T, then they always meet (probability 1).

Otherwise, each triangle has legs of length (T - Z/60). So the area of one triangle is (1/2)*(T - Z/60)^2, and there are two such triangles.

Therefore, the non-meeting area = 2 * (1/2) * (T - Z/60)^2 = (T - Z/60)^2.

Hence, the meeting area = total area - non-meeting area = T^2 - (T - Z/60)^2.
