Looking at this problem, I need to find the maximum number of people the elevator can deliver to parking within time T.

Key observations:
1. The elevator starts at parking (basement, floor 0)
2. It needs to go up to pick people from floors 1 to N, then return to parking
3. The elevator has capacity C
4. Going up/down one floor takes P seconds
5. We want to maximize people delivered in T seconds

Strategy:
- The elevator should pick people starting from lower floors (since it needs to go up anyway)
- For each "trip", the elevator goes up to some maximum floor, picks people (greedily from lower floors up to that max floor, respecting capacity C), and returns to parking
- Each trip to floor `max_floor` takes: `2 * max_floor * P` seconds (up and down)
- We want to fit as many complete trips as possible within time T

Approach:
1. Try different strategies: the elevator goes up to floor `i` as its maximum, picks people greedily from floors 1 to i (as many as capacity allows, prioritizing lower floors), returns to parking
2. We can do this multiple times until time runs out
3. For each maximum floor, calculate the time needed for one trip and see how many complete trips we can do
