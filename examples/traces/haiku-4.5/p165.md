Looking at this problem, I need to:

1. Find an arrangement of N players such that for any contiguous subsequence of K players, their total height H satisfies |H - 2000*K| ≤ 100 (10 cm in mm)
2. Heights are given in meters and range from 1.95 to 2.05 meters
3. Average height is exactly 2000 mm

Key insight: If I convert heights to deviations from 2000 mm, each player has a deviation in the range [-50, +50] mm. The constraint becomes: for any contiguous subsequence, the sum of deviations must have absolute value ≤ 100 mm.

Strategy: A greedy approach should work - arrange players so that we try to keep the running sum of deviations balanced. One effective method is to interleave players with positive deviations and negative deviations, or sort them in a way that prevents the prefix sum from growing too large.

Let me think about a simpler greedy approach:

- Try to arrange them so the prefix sums stay within [-100, 100]

Actually, a better strategy: place players alternating between those above and below average, or use a greedy algorithm that picks the next player that keeps prefix sums balanced.
