Let me think about this differently - if I have 2 black balls in every M consecutive balls, what's the maximum gap I can have between consecutive black balls?

If I have a black ball at position i, and the next black ball is at position j, then for any M consecutive balls to have at least 2 black balls, the gap between them can't be too large.

If there are more than M-1 positions between two black balls (i.e., j - i > M), then there would be a window of M consecutive balls with at most 1 black ball, which violates the constraint.

So the constraint is: **the gap between any two consecutive black balls must be at most M**.

In other words, if ball i is black, then at least one of balls i+1, i+2, ..., i+M must also be black.

- `dp[i]` = minimum dye needed to paint balls 0 to i, where ball i is painted black
- For each position i where we paint a ball black, the previous black ball must be at position j where `i - j <= M` (to satisfy the constraint)
