 * The Death Cube is a rectangular parallelepiped with dimensions X, Y, Z.
 * Cabins (cells) are at integer points (x, y, z) with 1 <= x <= X, 1 <= y <= Y, 1 <= z <= Z.
 * A passageway exists between two cabins if they share a face.
 * The goal is to find the shortest path that traverses all passageways,
 * starting and ending at a cabin on the outer surface.
 * This is a variation of the Chinese Postman Problem (Route Inspection Problem).
 * 
 * Let M be the total number of passageways (edges in the graph).
 * Let O be the set of nodes with odd degree.
 * The shortest walk that visits all edges and starts/ends at specific nodes s, e is:
 *   Length = M + min-weight perfect matching of the set (O delta {s, e}).
 * Since s and e must be on the outer surface, we minimize this over all s, e on the surface.
 * 
 * For a node (x, y, z), its degree is the number of its neighbors.
 * For L >= 2, f(i, L) = 1 if i=1 or i=L, and 2 if 1 < i < L.
 * For L = 1, f(i, L) = 0.
 * Degree d(x, y, z) = f(x, X) + f(y, Y) + f(z, Z).

Final check on $W(O \triangle \{s, e\})$:
If $s, e$ are on the surface, and there are both odd and even nodes on the surface:
- If $O_{odd} > 0, O_{even} > 0$:
  Pick $s \in O_{odd}, e \in O_{even}$. $O \triangle \{s, e\} = (O_{odd}-1) \cup (O_{even}-1)$.
  Min matching weight: $\max(O_{odd}-1, O_{even}-1)$.
- If $O_{even} = 0$:
  Pick $s, e \in O_{odd}$. $O \triangle \{s, e\} = O_{odd}-2$.
  Min matching weight: $O_{odd}-2$.
- If $O_{odd} = 0$:
  Pick $s, e \in O_{even}$. $O \triangle \{s, e\} = O_{even}-2$.
  Min matching weight: $O_{even}-2$.
