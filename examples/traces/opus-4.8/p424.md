Looking at this problem:

- "Beautiful" = no two simple cycles share exactly one edge (no loops).
- For a single block, the densest beautiful graph is K(2,m) (a generalized "theta" with all paths of length 2): every cycle is a 4-cycle through both hubs, and any two cycles share 0 or 2 edges — never exactly 1.
- K(2,m) on V vertices gives 2(V-2) = 2V-4 edges. Splitting into multiple blocks or adding triangles is never better for V≥4.
- Special small cases: V=3 → triangle (3 edges) beats 2V-4=2; V=2 → 1 edge; V=1 → 0 edges.

So:
- n=1: 0 edges
- n=2: 1 edge
- n=3: triangle, 3 edges
- n≥4: K(2,n-2), edges 2n-4

This matches all provided samples.
