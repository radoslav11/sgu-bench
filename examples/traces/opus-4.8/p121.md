Looking at this problem:

Each island with degree ≥ 2 needs at least one white and one black bridge. Islands with degree 1 can have any color.

Let me think of this as: each edge gets a color (1 or 2). Constraint: every vertex with degree ≥ 2 must have at least one edge of color 1 and at least one of color 2.

This is a classic problem solvable by finding for each vertex an Euler-path-like assignment. Approach: consider each connected component. We want to 2-color edges so each vertex of degree≥2 sees both colors.

A known technique: alternate colors along Euler trails. For each vertex, edges alternate colors as we traverse, ensuring balance. Let me build an Euler tour and color edges alternately.

The strategy: Add edges, find Euler circuits/paths in each component, color alternately. When walking an Euler path, alternating colors gives each vertex (except possibly path endpoints with odd degree) both colors. Need to handle odd-degree vertices carefully — pair them up so Euler trails work, but endpoints of degree 1 are fine.

A cleaner known solution: For graph with vertices of odd degree, connect them to a virtual node, making all even, find Euler circuits, color alternately. For degree-1 vertices it's fine. Let me implement: add virtual vertex 0 connected to all odd-degree vertices. Then every vertex even degree, find Euler circuits and alternate-color. Edges to virtual node are ignored in output. Alternating along a closed circuit of even length keeps balance.

Let me implement Euler circuit with alternating colors.
