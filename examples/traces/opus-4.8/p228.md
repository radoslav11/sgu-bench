Looking at this problem:

We have a regular N-gon with vertices numbered clockwise. We know two vertices N1 and N2. We need to find the center, then compute all vertices.

The angle between vertex i and vertex j (going around) is `2π/N * (steps)`. Since numbered clockwise, consecutive vertices differ by angle `-2π/N` in standard math orientation (or we can just figure out the center).

The two known points are both on the circle. The center is equidistant. The angular separation between N1 and N2 is `k * 2π/N` where `k = (N2 - N1)` mod N.

Let me set up: vertices at angles `θ0 - i*2π/N` (clockwise). Midpoint approach: find center.

The two points P1, P2 are on a circle. The center lies on the perpendicular bisector. The central angle between them is `α = k * 2π/N` where `k = (N2-N1+N) % N`. 

Distance between P1,P2 = `2R sin(α/2)`. The center is at distance `R` from midpoint... Actually distance from midpoint M to center = `R cos(α/2)`, perpendicular to P1P2.

Direction: clockwise numbering. Let's determine sign. The center direction from midpoint: rotate vector (P2-P1) by 90°. Two choices; pick based on clockwise orientation.

For clockwise numbering, going from N1 to N2 (increasing index by k steps clockwise). In screen coords where y up, clockwise means angle decreasing. The center should be positioned such that rotating P1 clockwise by α gives P2 around center.

Let me just try: center = M + perpendicular * (R cos(α/2)). Rotation by -α (clockwise) around center maps P1 to P2. Let me verify with sample to pick the sign of perpendicular.

Sample: N=4,N1=1,N2=3, P1=(1,0), P2=(1,2). k=2, α=π. center=M=(1,1), R=1. Vertices clockwise from angle of P1. P1 at angle: (1-1,0-1)=(0,-1) angle=-90°. Clockwise next subtract 90°: -180°→(-1,0)→(0,1). Matches vertex2=(0,1). Good.

So vertex i: angle = θ1 - (i-N1)*2π/N where rotation by -2π/N each clockwise step.

```cpp
#include <bits/stdc++.h>
using namespace std;
int main(){
    int N,N1,N2;
    double x1,y1,x2,y2;
    cin>>N>>N1>>N2>>x1>>y1>>x2>>y2;
    int k=((N2-N1)%N+N)%N;
    double PI=acos(-1.0);
    double alpha=k*2*PI/N; // central angle, clockwise from N1 to N2
    double mx=(x1+x2)/2, my=(y1+y2)/2;
    double dx=x2-x1, dy=y2-y1;
    double half=alpha/2;
    double R = sqrt(dx*dx+dy*dy)/(2*sin(half));
    double dlen = R*cos(half);
    double L=sqrt(dx*dx+dy*dy);
    double ux=dx/L, uy=dy/L;
    double cx=mx + uy*dlen;
    double cy=my - ux*dlen;
    double a1=atan2(y1-cy, x1-cx);
    vector<pair<double,double>> res(N+1);
    for(int i=1;i<=N;i++){
        double ang=a1 - (i-N1)*2*PI/N;
        res[i]={cx+R*cos(ang), cy+R*sin(ang)};
    }
    for(int i=1;i<=N;i++){
        double X=res[i].first, Y=res[i].second;
        printf("%.6f %.6f\n", X+0.0, Y+0.0);
    }
    return 0;
}
```
